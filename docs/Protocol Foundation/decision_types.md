NCS Decision Types v0.1
Purpose

Define the universal categories of decisions that structured intelligence can process.

A decision type defines:

the nature of the problem

evaluation criteria

risk profile

expected outputs

1️⃣ STRATEGIC DECISION

Long-term direction and positioning.

Examples:

product direction

market choice

system architecture orientation

Evaluation focus:

impact

optionality

alignment with goals

Risk nature:

high uncertainty

high consequence

2️⃣ TECHNICAL DECISION

Implementation method or system design.

Examples:

architecture choice

technology stack

data structure

Evaluation focus:

feasibility

complexity

maintainability

Risk nature:

operational failure

scalability limits

3️⃣ RESOURCE ALLOCATION DECISION

Distribution of limited assets.

Examples:

time allocation

budget distribution

compute usage

Evaluation focus:

efficiency

opportunity cost

sustainability

Risk nature:

waste

constraint amplification

4️⃣ RISK CONTROL DECISION

Actions to reduce uncertainty or prevent failure.

Examples:

safeguards

validation steps

rollback strategy

Evaluation focus:

severity reduction

probability reduction

Risk nature:

residual exposure

5️⃣ EXECUTION DECISION

Authorization of an action.

Examples:

deploy system

publish output

initiate process

Evaluation focus:

readiness

reversibility

safety

Risk nature:

immediate consequences

6️⃣ KNOWLEDGE VALIDATION DECISION

Determining truth or reliability.

Examples:

accept assumption

validate data source

confirm hypothesis

Evaluation focus:

evidence quality

confidence level

Risk nature:

false belief propagation

Status: Foundational Taxonomy
